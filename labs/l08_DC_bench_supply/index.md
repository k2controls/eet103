### Lab 8: Using a Bench DC Power Supply

**Objective:**  
In this lab, students will learn to use a bench DC power supply, focusing on setting and observing output voltage regulation and current limiting. Students will compare the bench power supply's behavior with that of batteries, particularly how the output voltage remains regulated under different loads and how current limiting protects the circuit.

**Materials Needed:**  
- Bench DC power supply (adjustable)
- 100-ohm resistor (1/4 watt)
- 1 K-ohm resistor (1/4 watt)
- Breadboard and connecting wires
- Digital Multimeter (DMM)

### Pre-Lab Preparation:
1. Review the concepts of regulated voltage and current limiting in DC power supplies.
2. Recall your previous experience using batteries as power sources and how a change in load affected the terminal voltage.

### Procedure:
#### Part 1: Understanding the Bench Power Supply
1. **Set Up the Power Supply:**
   - Connect the bench DC power supply to the breadboard.
   - Set the output voltage to 5 volts without connecting a load. Adjust the current limit to 100 mA initially.
   - Measure the output voltage using the DMM to verify it is set to 5V.

2. **Observe Voltage Regulation with Load:**
   - Connect the 1 K-ohm resistor across the power supply terminals.
   - Measure and record the voltage across the resistor and the current flowing through the circuit using the DMM.
   - Observe and record whether the output voltage remains stable at 5 volts.

3. **Introduce a Higher Load:**
   - Replace the 1 K-ohm resistor with a 100-ohm resistor.
   - Predict what will happen when using this resistor given the 100 mA current limit.
   - Connect the resistor and observe the behavior of the power supply, focusing on the displayed current and voltage.
   - Record the voltage and current values, noting if the power supply enters current limiting mode.

#### Part 2: Exploring Current Limiting
1. **Adjust Current Limit:**
   - Increase the current limit to 200 mA and observe how the circuit behaves with the 100-ohm resistor connected.
   - Record the voltage and current values and note any changes from the previous setup.

2. **Limit Investigation:**
   - Decrease the current limit back to 50 mA and observe how the power supply responds when using the 100-ohm resistor.
   - Record observations about the voltage drop and how the current is maintained at 50 mA.

3. **Compare with Battery Behavior:**
   - Reflect on how the bench power supply’s behavior differs from your earlier experiences with batteries.
   - Discuss how the power supply maintains the current limit by adjusting voltage, unlike a battery where terminal voltage would drop with increased load.

### Analysis:
- What effect does changing the current limit have on the circuit with a 100-ohm resistor?
- How does the bench power supply ensure the current stays within the set limit?
- Why might current limiting be important in protecting a circuit or components?

### Conclusion:
Summarize your findings on the differences between using a bench power supply and batteries as power sources. Explain how current limiting provides an advantage in circuit testing and design.

### Submission Requirements:
- A video demonstration showing the setup and measurements taken during Part 2.
- Completed lab report with recorded measurements and answers to analysis questions.
- Include a discussion on how this experience with the bench power supply can be applied to future circuit designs.
