### Lab 9: Introduction to the Oscilloscope

**Objective:**  
Students will learn the fundamentals of using a digital oscilloscope, focusing on displaying and analyzing square, triangle, and sine waveforms using a signal generator. The lab will cover using two-channel inputs, adjusting vertical and horizontal scales, and using the oscilloscope's basic measurement functions.

**Materials Needed:**  
- Digital oscilloscope with two channels
- Signal generator (capable of generating square, triangle, and sine waves)
- BNC cables and connectors
- Probes for the oscilloscope

### Pre-Lab Preparation:
1. Review the main controls of an oscilloscope: vertical (volts/div), horizontal (time/div), and trigger settings.
2. Familiarize yourself with the signal generator’s basic functions, such as setting waveform type, frequency, and amplitude.

### Procedure:
#### Part 1: Setting Up the Oscilloscope
1. **Default Settings and Auto Setup:**
   - Turn on the oscilloscope and use the "Default Setup" or "Auto Scale" function to reset the scope to its default settings.
   - Connect the signal generator to Channel 1 using a BNC cable. Set the signal generator to output a 1 kHz sine wave at 1V peak-to-peak.

2. **Basic Display Adjustments:**
   - Adjust the **vertical scale** (volts/div) until the waveform is clearly visible, filling about 5–6 divisions vertically on the screen.
   - Adjust the **horizontal scale** (time/div) to display 2–3 full cycles of the waveform.

#### Part 2: Displaying Different Waveforms
1. **Sine Wave Display:**
   - Observe and record the sine wave displayed on the oscilloscope.
   - Use the **measure** function to determine the peak-to-peak voltage, frequency, and period.
   - Adjust the **vertical position** to center the waveform, then adjust the **trigger level** to stabilize the display.

2. **Square and Triangle Wave Display:**
   - Change the output of the signal generator to a 1 kHz square wave.
   - Repeat the steps above to adjust the display and measure the waveform parameters.
   - Switch to a 1 kHz triangle wave and repeat the process, noting any differences in the waveform shapes and measurements.

#### Part 3: Using Dual Channels
1. **Connecting a Second Signal:**
   - Connect a second waveform output (e.g., square wave) to Channel 2 of the oscilloscope.
   - Adjust the vertical scale for both channels to display both signals clearly on the screen.
   - Practice adjusting the **trigger** to stabilize one of the waveforms while observing the other.

2. **Comparing Signals:**
   - Use the oscilloscope’s **cursors** or **measure** functions to compare the frequencies of the two waveforms.
   - Record your observations about how different waveform types (sine, square, triangle) appear on the display.

### Analysis:
- How do adjustments to the vertical and horizontal controls affect the displayed waveform?
- What differences do you notice between sine, square, and triangle waves?
- Why is triggering important when displaying waveforms, and how does adjusting the trigger level impact the display?

### Conclusion:
Summarize your understanding of using a digital oscilloscope to display and measure waveforms. Discuss any challenges encountered when adjusting the oscilloscope settings and how you addressed them.

### Submission Requirements:
- A video demonstration of the setup and display of all three waveforms.
- A completed lab report including screenshots of the oscilloscope display for each waveform type and answers to analysis questions.
